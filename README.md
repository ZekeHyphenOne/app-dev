# Marvel
: Marvel is ==a premier American media and entertainment company==, widely recognized for its extensive universe of comic book characters and its massive multimedia presence, particularly through the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Founded in 1939 as Timely Publications, it rebranded as Marvel Comics in 1961 and was acquired by The Walt Disney Company in 2009. 

Here is an overview of key Marvel information:

## Marvel Comics
- **Origins:** Created by Martin Goodman, the company found early success with characters like Namor the Sub-Mariner and the Human Torch.

- **The Marvel Age (1960s):** Stan Lee, Jack Kirby, and Steve Ditko revolutionized comics by introducing flawed, relatable heroes, starting with The Fantastic Four #1 in 1961.

- **Key Characters:** Spider-Man, Wolverine, The Hulk, Captain America, Iron Man, Thor, Black Panther, Doctor Strange, and Captain Marvel.

- **Iconic Teams:** The Avengers, X-Men, Fantastic Four, and Guardians of the Galaxy.

- **Recent Developments:** In June 2024, Marvel unveiled a new logo for its comics division, mirroring the style used by Marvel Studios.

## Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU)
- **Launch:** The MCU began with Iron Man (2008), establishing a shared, interconnected universe of films and TV series.

- **Structure:** MCU content is organized into "Phases" that make up larger "Sagas" (e.g., The Infinity Saga, The Multiverse Saga).

## Interesting Facts
- The "No-Prize": A tradition where Marvel gives public recognition to fans who find clever explanations for contradictions in storylines.

- Stan Lee Cameos: The late Stan Lee appeared in almost every Marvel film until his passing in 2018.

- Hulk's Color: The Incredible Hulk was originally intended to be gray, but printing issues led to him becoming green.

- Spider-Man Rights: Due to 1990s bankruptcy, Marvel sold the film rights to Spider-Man to Sony, though he now appears in the MCU via licensing agreements.
 
## Recent News (2026)
- As of April 2026, Marvel has been affected by Disney company-wide layoffs, which have impacted its comic division, visual development, and corporate departments.

- Spider-Man: Beyond the Spider-Verse stills were recently unveiled in April 2026.
